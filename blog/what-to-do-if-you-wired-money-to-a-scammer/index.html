What To Do If You Wired Money to a Scammer

Updated April 2026 - 7 min read

Time is critical. Call your bank NOW before reading further. Every minute counts with wire recalls.

Wire transfer scams are devastating because the money moves fast. But you have more options than your bank may admit upfront.

Step 1 - Call Your Bank Immediately

  • Ask for the wire transfer department or fraud team directly
  • Say: I need to place a wire recall request on a fraudulent transfer immediately
  • Provide the wire reference number, amount, date, and destination bank
  • Ask them to send a SWIFT recall message to the receiving bank
  • Get a case number and names of everyone you speak with

Step 2 - Contact the Receiving Bank

If you know the destination bank, call them and ask to freeze the recipient account pending a fraud investigation. Banks have legal authority to freeze funds when fraud is reported directly.

Step 3 - File Reports

  • FBI IC3 at ic3.gov with full wire details
  • FTC at reportfraud.ftc.gov
  • Local police for a report number
  • FinCEN for amounts over $10,000 at fincen.gov

Step 4 - Contact Your State Banking Regulator

File a complaint with your state banking regulator. This puts official pressure on your bank to cooperate with the recall. Banks take these complaints very seriously.

Step 5 - Send a Formal Demand Letter

Write a certified letter to your bank citing the Electronic Fund Transfer Act and requesting a written explanation of all steps taken. This creates a paper trail essential for any legal action.

Recovery odds by timing

Within 24 hours: 30 to 50 percent recovery possible. Within 72 hours: 10 to 25 percent. After 1 week: under 10 percent but still pursue all reporting channels.

If the Money Went Overseas

International wire recoveries are harder but not impossible. The FBI IC3 has relationships with foreign law enforcement and financial intelligence units. Always file that report regardless of destination.
